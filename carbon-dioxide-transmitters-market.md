<p><p>The carbon dioxide transmitters market encompasses two primary types: infrared and heat conduction. Infrared transmitters utilize infrared light absorption to measure CO2 concentrations, offering high accuracy and responsiveness. In contrast, heat conduction transmitters assess CO2 levels by measuring changes in thermal conductivity of the air, which is less common but can be effective in specific applications. Both types are crucial for monitoring air quality in various settings, including industrial, commercial, and residential environments, enhancing safety and energy efficiency.</p></p>
