<p><p>The flavor enhancers market comprises several key types, including acidulants, hydrolyzed vegetable proteins, glutamates, and yeast extracts. Acidulants, such as citric acid, provide a tangy taste, enhancing overall flavor profiles. Hydrolyzed vegetable proteins contribute savory notes through umami, often found in processed foods. Glutamates, including monosodium glutamate (MSG), intensify flavors and are widely used in snacks and seasonings. Yeast extracts offer rich, complex flavors, making them popular in soups and sauces, enhancing both taste and aroma.</p></p>
